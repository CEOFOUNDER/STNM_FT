The Problem

AI in Finance rarely fails because the technology lacks potential.

It fails when organisations move too quickly into tools, pilots and isolated use cases without creating the conditions for adoption, trust, sequencing, control and change.

01

Ambition without adoption

Teams see the opportunity, but hesitate to rely on AI when stakes are high.

02

Pilots without a roadmap

Disconnected use cases create activity, but not trusted, measurable Finance value.

03

Tools without operating model change

AI only lands when workflows, controls, roles and decision rights change together.

Method

Define the roadmap. Land the change. Govern delivery.

The method is grounded in direct interaction with hundreds of Finance leaders and hands-on involvement in large transformation programmes. It connects AI ambition to Finance outcomes, adoption risk and programme oversight.

  • Separate productivity opportunities from decision-sensitive workflows.
  • Prioritise by value, feasibility, risk, data readiness and adoption effort.
  • Embed human-in-the-loop control where judgement matters.
  • Track value through disciplined programme oversight.

Agentic Finance

Build AI agents around how Finance work actually happens.

Generic tools are not enough. Finance needs AI designed around organisational context, quality standards, controls and continuous feedback.

01

Absorb organisational context

Map workflows, policies, process variation, exception handling and institutional knowledge.

02

Specify agent behaviour

Translate context into clear instructions, outputs, escalation rules and quality guardrails.

03

Deploy and iterate

Embed agents into real workflows, capture failures and turn feedback into better performance.

Change Risk

Every step has a change risk. Manage it deliberately.

Misalignment

Create a shared case for change across CFO, FP&A, controllership, treasury, tax and business finance.

Poor prioritisation

Use a common lens for value, feasibility, risk, data readiness, adoption and process redesign.

Exclusion

Design AI with practitioners, not around them. Capture where judgement and quality really sit.

Loss of confidence

Make feedback visible, classify errors, improve guardrails and show teams that the system learns.

How-To Guides

Typical work packages for AI-enabled Finance transformation.

These are practical building blocks for Finance leaders shaping a future-state function, operating model and AI-enabled automation roadmap.

01

Unite Process, Architecture and Change

Create one Finance transformation agenda across process ownership, technology architecture and adoption.

  • Map current initiatives, owners and overlaps.
  • Agree shared outcomes, governance and decision rights.
  • Create a single transformation narrative for Finance leadership.
02

Translate strategy into sequenced roadmaps

Turn Finance strategy into a prioritised roadmap for AI automation, process change and future capabilities.

  • Assess use cases by value, feasibility, data readiness and risk.
  • Separate quick wins from strategic capabilities.
  • Define dependencies, milestones and measurable benefits.
03

Design the end-to-end future state

Define what Finance should look like by 2030 across teams, processes, controls, data and platforms.

  • Document target workflows and decision rights.
  • Assess impact across Finance teams.
  • Identify where human judgement must remain central.
04

Lead cross-functional Finance design

Bring Treasury, FP&A, Business Partnering, Controllership and Finance Operations into one design rhythm.

  • Run design workshops with clear decision logs.
  • Resolve hand-offs, data dependencies and ownership gaps.
  • Create a shared view of value, risk and adoption effort.
05

Define the 2030 deliverables

Create the artefacts that make the Finance future state concrete enough to govern and deliver.

  • Future-state blueprint and roadmap.
  • Operating model and capability model.
  • Benefits case, risks, controls and adoption plan.
06

Advise the Finance Executive Committee

Convert complex transformation design into clear executive decisions and board-ready progress updates.

  • Prepare decision papers and executive storylines.
  • Track dependencies, risks and unresolved choices.
  • Build a quarterly CFO update rhythm.
07

Lead the transformation organisation

Turn a direct leadership team and wider Finance population into active stewards of the change.

  • Clarify ownership by function and workstream.
  • Set adoption roles for leaders and practitioners.
  • Monitor engagement, capability and resistance.
08

Align with enterprise technology and data

Make sure Finance design fits enterprise platforms, architecture standards and data governance.

  • Partner with enterprise architecture and data teams.
  • Assess platform readiness and integration needs.
  • Define common standards for AI-enabled workflows.
09

Govern implementation through to value

Use programme oversight to connect roadmap delivery to adoption, controls and measurable outcomes.

  • Track milestones, risks, issues and dependencies.
  • Measure adoption, quality, time saved and controls strengthened.
  • Use feedback loops to improve the roadmap continuously.
