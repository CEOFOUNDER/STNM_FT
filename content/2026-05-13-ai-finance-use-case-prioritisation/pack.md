## Core Problem
Many Finance functions are now surrounded by AI opportunity. Every team can identify a task that could be automated, accelerated or improved.

The problem is not a shortage of ideas.

The problem is prioritisation.

Without a clear method, Finance quickly creates disconnected pilots: one in FP&A, one in reporting, one in close, one in procurement finance, one in controls, one in business partnering. Each may look useful in isolation, but together they often fail to create a coherent transformation path.

The result is pilot sprawl: activity without scale, excitement without adoption, and technology without measurable operating model change.

## Referenceable Use Case
A common starting point is management reporting and variance commentary.

Many Finance teams still spend significant time producing recurring packs, reconciling numbers, explaining movements, drafting commentary and answering follow-up questions from business stakeholders.

AI can help by:

- Drafting first-pass variance commentary
- Identifying unusual movements and possible drivers
- Checking whether commentary is consistent with source data
- Highlighting missing explanations
- Preparing questions for Finance Business Partners to review
- Standardising the quality of recurring reporting outputs

This is often a good early use case because the workflow is frequent, visible, measurable and usually already has defined review points. It can reduce manual effort without removing human accountability.

## Measurable Achievement Logic
The value case should be expressed through practical Finance measures, not vague claims.

Typical measures include:

- Hours saved per reporting cycle
- Reduction in rework between draft and final pack
- Faster time from data close to management insight
- Improved consistency of commentary across business units
- Fewer manual checks required before review
- Better visibility of exceptions and unexplained movements
- Increased Finance Business Partner time spent on challenge and decision support

The strongest cases connect productivity gains to operating rhythm: faster monthly reporting, better business conversations, stronger review discipline and more time for insight.

## Framework
Use a prioritisation lens that scores each use case against six dimensions:

1. Value
What measurable business or Finance outcome will improve?

2. Feasibility
Can the workflow realistically be changed with the current process, systems and data?

3. Risk
What could go wrong if the AI output is incomplete, biased, wrong or overtrusted?

4. Data Readiness
Is the underlying data accessible, sufficiently clean and governed?

5. Adoption Effort
How much behaviour change, training and process redesign will be required?

6. Scaling Potential
Can the use case become a reusable capability across Finance, or is it a narrow local improvement?

The strongest early candidates are usually high-value, repeatable workflows with clear human review points, manageable risk and visible pain.

## Emerging Practice
The emerging best practice is to stop selecting AI use cases purely through ideation workshops.

Instead, Finance leaders should combine:

- Leadership alignment on Finance outcomes
- Workflow discovery with practitioners
- Process and data readiness assessment
- Risk and control review
- Adoption planning
- Benefits logic
- A sequenced roadmap

This creates a transformation path, not a collection of experiments.

## Practical Actions
Start with a Finance leadership workshop focused on outcomes, not tools.

Ask:

- Where does Finance spend too much time producing, checking or explaining information?
- Which workflows are frequent enough for improvement to matter?
- Where do current outputs suffer from inconsistency, delay or rework?
- Which decisions rely on these outputs?
- Where must human judgement remain central?
- Which use cases could build trust early?
- Which use cases should wait until data, controls or process ownership improve?

Then score candidate use cases through the six-dimension lens and split them into three groups:

- Start now: low-to-medium risk, high-frequency, measurable productivity or insight opportunities
- Prepare next: valuable use cases that require data, process or control improvements first
- Defer: high-risk or poorly defined use cases where the organisation is not ready

## Change Risk
The main change risk is that teams experience AI prioritisation as a top-down technology exercise.

If that happens, adoption will be weak. Teams may comply with pilots, but they will not fully trust or embed the new way of working.

To manage this, involve Finance leaders and practitioners early. Make the prioritisation criteria transparent. Show why some use cases are being selected first and why others are being deferred. Be explicit that the goal is not to replace Finance judgement, but to free Finance capacity for better analysis, challenge and business partnering.
