# LinkedIn Enquiry Reply Playbook

Purpose: respond to LinkedIn enquiries professionally, protect Gilles' time, detect commercial potential, and route valuable enquiries towards the free AI Finance Playbook assistant and the paid AI Finance Diagnostic.

This playbook is designed for draft-and-approve use first. Fully unattended auto-send is not recommended unless the workflow is operated through a LinkedIn-approved tool.

## Core Principles

Every reply should be:

- Welcoming
- Professional
- Brief
- Commercially bounded
- Aligned to See The Next Move's methodology
- Clear that tailored advice starts with the paid diagnostic

Do not offer unpaid calls.

Do not over-explain.

Do not debate with low-fit enquiries.

Do not provide detailed consulting advice inside LinkedIn messages.

## Potential Signals

High potential if the message mentions:

- CFO, Finance Director, Finance leadership, ExCo, Board or senior Finance role
- AI in Finance, Finance Transformation, digital transformation or automation
- FP&A, controllership, reporting, close, reconciliations, treasury, tax, procurement finance or business partnering
- Roadmap sequencing, operating model, architecture, governance, adoption, change management or programme oversight
- Process mining, process mapping, time and motion, standardisation or benefits realisation
- Banking, insurance, regulated industries, large enterprise or complex global organisations
- Paid diagnostic, advisory, workshop, project, transformation support or speaking engagement

Possible potential if the message:

- Asks a serious capability question but lacks budget or seniority detail
- Comes from a practitioner in Finance, consulting, technology or transformation
- Mentions a tool, vendor or emerging practice but not a clear buying need
- Could become a useful content idea or relationship

Low potential if the message:

- Is generic networking with no Finance or AI context
- Asks for free mentoring, free consulting or broad career advice
- Is a vendor pitch unrelated to the methodology
- Requests a call without a clear business problem

No potential / time waster if the message:

- Is spam
- Is unrelated to Finance, AI, transformation or advisory
- Asks for personal favours, speculative unpaid work or irrelevant promotion
- Tries to consume time without a clear problem or commercial route

## Classification

Use four categories:

1. High Potential
Reply with paid diagnostic as the recommended next step, with free assistant as preparation.

2. Possible Potential
Reply with the free assistant first and paid diagnostic if tailored advice is needed.

3. Low Potential
Politely redirect to free resources and close the loop.

4. No Potential
Politely stop the interaction and give one useful self-serve direction.

## Escalation Rules

Escalate for manual review if:

- The person appears to be a CFO, Finance Director, transformation sponsor or senior leader
- The enquiry references a live project, budget, role, procurement process or formal opportunity
- The person asks for a speaking engagement, partnership or media contribution
- The message is sensitive, legal, reputational or personally complex
- The correct answer is unclear

## Do Not Say

- "Happy to jump on a quick call"
- "Let's have a free consultation"
- "I can help with that in detail here"
- "Send me everything and I will review"
- "No problem, I can give you some advice"

## Preferred Language

- "A good first step is..."
- "If you need tailored advice..."
- "The best next step is the paid diagnostic..."
- "This is exactly the type of question the AI Finance Playbook was built for..."
- "I may not be the right person for this..."

## Quality Gate Before Sending

Before any reply is sent, check:

- Is it welcoming?
- Is it professional?
- Does it avoid giving away unpaid advisory work?
- Does it classify the enquiry correctly?
- Does it move potential buyers towards the paid diagnostic?
- Does it politely close low-value enquiries?
- Is it short enough for LinkedIn?
- Is it in British English?
